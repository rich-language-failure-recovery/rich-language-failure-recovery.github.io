RACER: Rich Language-Guided Failure Recovery Policies for Imitation Learning

Yinpei Dai* 1, Jayjun Lee* 2, Nima Fazeli2, Joyce Chai1,
*Equal Contribution
1Department of Computer Science and Engineering, University of Michigan, 2Robotics Department, University of Michigan

Abstract

Developing robust and correctable and interactive visuomotor policies for object manipulation is challenging due to the lack of mechanisms for self-recovery from failures and the limitations of simple language instructions in guiding robot actions. To address these issues, we introduce Rich languAge-guided failure reCovERy (RACER) policy, a supervisor-actor framework that combines failure recovery data with rich language descriptions to enhance robot control. RACER features a vision-language model (VLM) that acts as an online supervisor, providing detailed guidance for error correction and task execution, and a language-conditioned visuomotor policy as an actor to predict next action. By augmenting the RLbench dataset with fine-grained language annotations and recovery trajectories, we demonstrate how our method significantly improves policy robustness and sim-to-real transfer capabilities. Specifically, our experimental results demonstrate that RACER outperforms the state-of-the-art Robotic View Transformer (RVT) in various scenarios, including standard long-horizon tasks, dynamic goal changes, and zero-shot unseen tasks, achieving superior performance in both simulated and real-world environments.
